# Living Worlds Design

Status: direction approved; written specification pending operator review
Date: 2026-07-14
Source: `victor-software-house/frontend-design-corpus@33c360659fed449fc849d4ed651bb3a856b76f12`
Repository: `victor-software-house/frontend-design-system`

## Purpose

Build a modern React design system that preserves the intelligence and quality of the pinned corpus without turning its works into screenshots, templates, or exhibits inside a new gallery identity.

The public experience is the corpus made live and connected. Stable packages provide invisible foundations. Copy-owned registry source provides world-specific compositions. Complete works remain authored exemplars rather than stable APIs.

## Become, then elevate

The work proceeds through two distinct gates.

### Gate 1: become it

Before changing the corpus, the translation must demonstrate that it has absorbed the original's subject, voice, hierarchy, pacing, typography, color expenditure, interaction manners, and authored moment. React, shared tokens, and reusable behavior must not lower its visual or literary quality.

This gate is passed only when the live translation feels native to the original world and the operator approves the complete journey by eye.

### Gate 2: elevate from within

After fidelity is established, the work must go beyond preservation. Elevation may add continuity between related pages, deeper interaction, richer state, stronger responsive composition, better accessibility, clearer consequence, or a new authored moment that the world's own logic implies.

Elevation must not arrive from external trend language, gallery chrome, fashionable effects, or generic component-library aesthetics. It must feel as though the original world discovered more of itself.

Every elevated change must answer three questions:

1. Which existing belief or pattern does this extend?
2. Why could this improvement belong only to this subject or world?
3. Does the original remain visibly present in the result without becoming a museum piece?

A migration that only reproduces is incomplete. A redesign that innovates before fidelity is counterfeit.

## Governing correction

Two gallery directions were rejected before this design:

1. A curation workbench made process UI more prominent than the work.
2. An editorial portfolio made the originals into screenshot plates beneath another visual identity.

Both failed for the same reason: the gallery claimed authorship over works that must remain sovereign.

The replacement model is therefore not a gallery shell. It is a federation of living worlds with two summonable instruments:

- **Atlas** — travel between worlds and studies.
- **System lens** — reveal shared machinery inside the active work.

Both instruments disappear completely when not in use.

## Corpus laws

### Subject precedes system

Each work begins with a subject, audience, tension, and mood. Components are selected only after the native representation of the problem is understood.

### Content and interface are one material

The strongest language defines behavior and hierarchy. The boat batches delivery. The bench has a real queue. The wall obeys weather. The ledger recognizes a hand. Removing the language changes the product.

### One authored moment carries each work

Every approved work needs a composition that could not be exchanged with another without breaking meaning: the sewn spine, three roads, listening wall, gate rail, anomalous ember series, plotted light, or correspondence window.

The surrounding interface remains quiet enough for that moment to govern.

### Repetition creates a world, not a template

Related works share memory, manners, nouns, and consequences while retaining distinct silhouettes. Consistency is continuity, not identical cards.

### Typography has dramatic roles

Typography is not one global family or scale:

- display serif carries ceremony or conviction
- text serif carries sustained thought and intimacy
- sans carries operation and navigation
- mono carries evidence, provenance, time, identifiers, and machine state
- italic carries voice, qualification, or material character

Each world maps these roles to approved faces.

### Density follows the act

A catalogue is dense because lookup is the work. A decision minute is spacious because consequence must settle. A console is tight because scanning matters. No global density system may flatten those differences.

### Quiet is constructed

Quiet comes from measure, pacing, hairlines, disciplined color, controlled emphasis, and meaningful empty space. It is not absence of design.

### Color is expenditure

- one blue may point across a reading surface
- one ember identifies the exceptional series or decision
- green seals resolution
- amber holds a gate
- dark walls remain neutral charcoal
- moonlight accents remain muted
- categorical rainbow palettes do not survive

### Ornament informs or becomes pure form

An ornament must add meaning, support use, or remain honestly abstract. It must not repeat adjacent information.

### States preserve dignity and work

- Empty knows the room and offers one door.
- Loading names the labor and reveals real progress when known.
- Error names the failure, states what was kept, and places repair within reach.
- Success closes the loop and yields attention back.

### Interaction obeys the world's time

The product may batch, wait for weather, ask a bench, or rest a ledger. Timing belongs to the world and must not be replaced with generic spinners or artificial immediacy.

### Evidence leads to judgment

Charts, tables, timelines, and ledgers lead to a decision, gate, repair, or explicit unresolved question. Display without consequence is insufficient.

### Dark mode is a second composition

Dark mode is not inversion. Each world maps semantic roles into a neutral-charcoal night register with restrained accents. A work may be dark-only when its subject and use justify it.

### Shared mechanics remain subordinate

Theme, focus, reduced motion, state machines, sorting, filtering, responsive rules, and chart behavior may be shared. Their visual expression remains local.

## Public experience

### Entry

The root route enters directly into the live Correspondence world. It does not present a gallery hero or explain the project before the visitor encounters the work.

The default entry is the approved Correspondence landing composition. From there, native product navigation reaches the thread, Mat, Desk, and API.

### Houses and studies

The public catalogue has two conceptual levels:

- **Houses** — connected works with real shared memory and product continuity.
- **Studies** — sovereign works whose register should not be forced into a house.

This distinction appears only in the Atlas and metadata. It does not stamp a common visual treatment onto the works.

### Native world navigation

Each house owns its navigation language and visual grammar.

Examples:

- Correspondence may navigate through the desk, mat, letters, and ledger.
- Passerine may navigate through the press, new hand, materials, and commission.
- San Brizio may navigate through the studio record, watch, year, and seasons.

A world may share state across routes when that continuity strengthens the fiction and demonstrates real product behavior.

### Crossing worlds

Travel within a world may use a restrained View Transition when motion preferences permit it.

Travel between worlds cuts cleanly. One world must not morph into another as if palette, typography, and material were interchangeable skins.

## Atlas

The Atlas is a navigation instrument, not a homepage.

### Behavior

- Available from every public route through a small labelled trigger.
- Openable with `⌘K` on macOS and `Ctrl+K` elsewhere; the shortcut does not fire while the visitor is typing in an editable control.
- Rendered as an accessible dialog with focus containment and return.
- Lists houses first and studies second.
- Supports type-ahead search by title, subject, capability, and world.
- Shows the visitor's current location without ranking works by popularity.
- Closes completely and leaves no persistent frame around the work.
- Has a dedicated route containing the same semantic index for deep links, assistive technology, and search engines.

### Visual restraint

- text-first
- no screenshot cards
- no oversized project slogan
- no decorative portfolio grid
- no inherited palette mimicry
- no explanation that competes with titles

If the Atlas starts becoming a visual identity, reduce it to a cleaner textual index.

## System lens

The system lens reveals implementation and reuse in context without altering the work by default.

### Lens states

1. **Off** — the authored work is fully sovereign.
2. **Foundations** — reveal semantic roles such as surface, text, measure, type role, focus, and motion.
3. **Behavior** — identify owned primitives and state contracts in the live interface.
4. **Source** — show package ownership, registry ownership, exemplar-local source, tests, and Storybook links.

### Annotation boundary

The lens marks only genuine shared machinery. It must not label a one-off authored moment as a reusable component merely because it has a DOM boundary.

Examples that remain authored composition:

- sewn-spine hero
- three-road decision comparison
- listening wall sensor field
- creed band
- plotted-light essay figure
- gate rail

### Accessibility

The same lens information is available as a structured drawer or page outline. Visual overlays are supplementary and never the only representation.

## Reuse architecture

### Stable token package

The token package owns semantic roles and constraints, not one costume:

- neutral surface and text roles
- border hierarchy
- focus roles
- status and state semantics
- motion durations and easing families
- reading measures
- spacing foundations
- Single House data palette
- optional font-face exports
- type-role contracts

Worlds map their own values into these roles.

### Stable React package

The React package owns difficult behavior behind minimal public APIs:

- theme script, provider, toggle, and preference resolution
- accessible switch and segmented choice
- disclosure and dialog behavior
- live-region and quiet-save announcement
- persistent step-machine state
- form error association
- table sorting and filtering behavior
- chart focus, scales, direct labels, and theme redraw

It does not export generic domain compositions such as `Card`, `DashboardShell`, `PageTemplate`, `DataTable`, or `Wizard` in the first release.

### Copy-owned registry

The registry owns compositions whose meaning depends on a world or subject:

- Correspondence furniture and letter treatments
- Passerine thread progress and material choices
- San Brizio instruments and records
- scholarly catalogue treatment
- chart report panel
- document decision structures

Consumers copy and own this source. The registry protocol provides distribution, not a universal visual language.

### Authored exemplars

Full pages remain local to the gallery application. They may consume package foundations and registry source, but their complete composition is not a public API.

## Application architecture

### Route model

Initial routes:

```text
/
/houses/correspondence
/houses/correspondence/thread
/houses/correspondence/mat
/houses/correspondence/desk
/houses/correspondence/api
/houses/passerine
/houses/passerine/new-hand
/houses/passerine/commission
/houses/san-brizio/minute-14
/houses/san-brizio/watch
/houses/san-brizio/damp-year
/houses/san-brizio/seasons
/studies/:slug
/atlas
/frame/:slug
```

`/` resolves to the Correspondence entry route for the first proof. Every approved route is prerendered for GitHub Pages.

### Isolation

Public house routes render natively so related surfaces can share state and transitions.

Exemplar styles remain bounded through:

- world root attributes
- CSS Modules for local composition
- explicit cascade layers
- no global Tailwind utilities inside authored work
- token values mapped at the world root

The workbench and deterministic visual-test frame use `/frame/:slug` in an iframe so gallery tools cannot alter rendering.

### Metadata

Each public work records:

- title and slug
- house or study membership
- subject and register
- provenance at the full pinned SHA or native origin
- disposition
- theme policy
- print capability
- scenarios
- shared foundations used
- registry source used
- authored moments that must remain local
- approval date

Rejected works never enter public metadata.

### State and data

Fixtures are deterministic and local. State that demonstrates continuity may persist within a house session, but test scenarios can reset it explicitly.

The public app does not depend on a backend for the first release. Interactions demonstrate complete product behavior with deterministic client state.

### Failure behavior

- Unknown public routes resolve to the semantic Atlas index with a precise not-found message; they do not invent a generic branded error page.
- Missing or duplicate metadata, invalid provenance, unresolved frame routes, and lens annotations without declared ownership fail the build.
- A failed optional font load falls back to the declared role stack without blocking content or shifting controls outside their bounds.
- Deterministic fixtures expose authored error scenarios; unexpected runtime exceptions surface through a minimal recovery boundary and are treated as test failures.
- The public app makes no runtime network request for exemplar content, metadata, fonts, or fixtures.

## Content requirements

- Preserve or improve the specificity of the original prose.
- Do not replace authored content with neutral placeholders during migration.
- Re-author any weak source before integration.
- Keep world terminology consistent across every route, state, API example, error, and action.
- Do not use poetic labels when they reduce comprehension.
- Every migrated work states its subject tension and authored moment before implementation.

## First visual proof

Only the Correspondence house is built for the first approval cycle.

The visual proof is authored in a disposable workspace outside the target Git repository. Gate 1 is reviewed before Gate 2 begins. Only the operator-approved elevated result is then reimplemented cleanly in the target repository; rejected proof code, captures, and translations never enter its object database or branches.

### Review sequence

1. **Fidelity proof** — live React translation of the five Correspondence surfaces, with shared machinery visually subordinate.
2. **Fidelity review** — full-height desktop and mobile, light and dark, plus the complete route journey.
3. **Elevation proof** — add only improvements derived from Correspondence's existing beliefs: continuity, preserved state, richer consequences, and one new authored moment.
4. **Elevation review** — compare against both the pinned originals and the approved fidelity proof.
5. **Clean implementation** — reproduce the approved result in the target repository without importing temporary proof history or files.

### Scope

- live landing route
- live Elena and Tomaz thread
- live Mat
- live Desk
- live API reference
- native within-world navigation
- preserved theme preference
- one shared product-state thread where useful
- disappearing Atlas
- disappearing system lens
- direct frame routes for review

### Stop conditions

Stop and remove the proof when:

- Atlas or lens creates a persistent gallery silhouette
- related pages become visually uniform rather than continuous
- shared tokens erase a page's register
- content is shortened into generic product copy
- screenshot plates replace live work
- modern effects become the visual thesis
- the system lens overstates reuse
- mobile or dark treatment becomes secondary
- operator visual review rejects the result

A rejected proof does not enter the target repository's Git history, branches, snapshots, registry output, packages, or public deployment.

## Verification

### Mechanical

- strict TypeScript
- zero lint warnings and errors
- route prerender and GitHub Pages deep links
- keyboard navigation
- focus restoration
- reduced motion
- no horizontal overflow at 390×844
- deterministic theme behavior
- axe with no serious or critical violations
- ARIA structural snapshots
- direct frame loading
- no console or request failures

### Visual

Review full-height live journeys, not isolated screenshots only:

- desktop light
- desktop dark
- mobile light
- mobile dark
- relevant interaction states
- Atlas open and closed
- system lens off and each active layer

Mechanical success cannot approve taste. Operator review remains the gate.

## Non-goals for the first proof

- no Storybook catalogue as the public gallery
- no component inventory homepage
- no card-grid browser
- no screenshot-led portfolio
- no generic admin curation UI
- no package publication
- no broad primitive extraction
- no migration of all 23 works
- no visual standardization across worlds

## Decision record

The operator selected **Living worlds with a summonable Atlas and system lens** on 2026-07-14.

A neutral anthology remains the failure-safe fallback for Atlas design. An adaptive gallery shell is rejected because it would make the shell another author competing with the corpus.
